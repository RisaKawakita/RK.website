CH.1 Summary

In the first chapter, it explains that there are already some processes developed. These days, the electronic devices are getting close to our body as a physical position. When the computer was born, it needed a dedicated room, but it has become smaller and more portable. The variety is increasing, and so-called wearable devices are attached to the body in the form of glasses or watches and from this rapid development, it is natural to think that eventually it will be integrated with the skin and enter the inside of our body. The first idea of "cyborg", which is the word came from 'Cybernetic Organisms' was established by Manfred Clynes band Nathan Kline, who are both working for the Dynamic Simulation Laboratory. At a distance from the debate over whether humans should become cyborgs, Andy Clark argues that "humans are naturally born as cyborgs" and that the link between the body and technology is a cyborg, but it is also a human nature that has been seen for a long time. What is special about the human brain, and what best describes the distinguishing features of human intelligence, is its ability to establish deep and complex relationships with abiotic constructs. However, this ability does not depend on the physical fusion of the wire-implant method, but rather on our openness to information processing fusion. As anyone who has ever felt as if they were thinking through the act of writing, this information processing fusion can be achieved without invading the body by any of the electric devices. In other words, what is important is not that the object enters the body, but that the object and the body interact and cooperate with each other.

Question 1

The traditional Klines and Clines definition of the cyborg is to connect biological organisms with the electronical devices, or the data processing, which the injected electronic devices uses the feedback signal from the body to automatically regulate wakefulness, metabolism, respiration, heart rate, and other physiological functions in the way adapted to the environment. On the other hand, Clark states that we are not becoming one with the electronic devices, instead we are already born as cyborgs, which we have an ability to establish deep and complex relationship with abiotic constructs that does not depend on the physical fusion of wire-implant scheme.
